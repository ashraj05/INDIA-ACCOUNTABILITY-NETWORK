# **India Accountability Network**

## **Community & Collaboration System Plan**

---

# **1\. Objective of Community Networks**

The purpose of creating social media communities is to:

1. Bring together people from different domains.

2. Encourage structured discussions about civic accountability.

3. Convert ideas into actionable platform features.

4. Document knowledge and insights for the open-source project.

5. Identify contributors and assign roles.

The community should act as a **national think tank \+ innovation lab for civic accountability**.

---

# **2\. Platform Ecosystem Structure**

Your ecosystem will have four main digital layers.

### **Layer 1 – GitHub (Core Project)**

Purpose:

* Host open-source code

* Maintain documentation

* Store architecture plans

* Track development tasks

The GitHub README should contain:

* vision document

* project roadmap

* community links

* contributor guide

* documentation links

---

### **Layer 2 – Knowledge Base (Notion / GitBook)**

Purpose:

Central structured documentation of:

* ideas

* research

* discussion summaries

* architecture plans

* civic metrics

* municipality frameworks

This becomes the **brain of the movement**.

Every discussion from communities must be summarized here.

---

### **Layer 3 – Discussion Communities**

Use platforms where people can discuss ideas.

Recommended platforms:

Discord (best for structured communities)  
 Telegram (easy onboarding)  
 LinkedIn group (for professionals)  
 Twitter/X community (for visibility)

---

### **Layer 4 – Public Awareness Channels**

These are outward-facing platforms.

Examples:

Twitter/X  
 Instagram  
 LinkedIn  
 YouTube

Purpose:

* attract volunteers

* share civic insights

* promote transparency

# **4\. Discussion Workflow**

To keep discussions productive, every discussion should follow a structure.

### **Step 1 – Idea Proposal**

A contributor posts an idea.

Example format:

Title  
 Problem  
 Proposed Solution  
 Expected Impact  
 Possible Challenges

---

### **Step 2 – Community Discussion**

Members discuss:

feasibility  
 implementation  
 risks

---

### **Step 3 – Idea Documentation**

A designated role called **Knowledge Curator** summarizes the discussion.

They record:

* key insights

* final proposal

* next steps

---

### **Step 4 – Documentation**

The summary is added to:

Notion knowledge base.

---

### **Step 5 – Development Planning**

If approved:

Product team converts the idea into:

Feature specifications.

Developers create:

GitHub issues.

---
